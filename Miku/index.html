Hatsune Miku

About Miku!

Hatsune Miku is a virtual pop star and Vocaloid software voicebank developed by Crypton Future Media, released in 2007. Her voice is synthesized using Yamaha’s Vocaloid technology, and she’s portrayed as a 16-year-old girl with long turquoise twin-tails. Miku quickly became a global icon thanks to her unique blend of music, tech, and fan creativity. She performs live as a 3D hologram, drawing crowds worldwide, and has collaborated with major artists and brands. Beyond music, she’s a symbol of digital culture and fan-driven innovation.

Key Points

  • Hatsune Miku is a Vocal Synthesizer

    Her voice is sampled from Japanese voice actress Saki Fujita.

  • Global Star

    Despite being a virtual singer, Miku has performed sold-out concerts around the world—with her 3D hologram projected on stage and a live band backing her up.

  • Collaborated with Real Artists

    She’s performed with real musicians like Lady Gaga (as her opening act) and Pharrell Williams, and has even been remixed by EDM producers like Porter Robinson.

  • Powered by Fans

    Her songs, dances, outfits, and art are often created by fans. She’s crowd-sourced creativity at its finest!

  • First Virtual Singer at Coachella

    Hatsune Miku was the first virtual singer to perform at Coachella, making her debut at the festival in 2020.
